How to talk to Career OS

You'll get more out of it if you use it like a conversation, not a search engine.

1

Start with your situation, not a question

Don't ask "how do I negotiate a raise?" Instead:

"I've been in my role for 3 years, doing work above my level. My manager keeps saying there's no budget. I don't know if I should push harder or start looking."
2

Be honest, not polished

This isn't a job interview. If your manager is awful, say that. If you're scared, say that. It works best with the real version.

3

Answer its questions

It'll ask things like "how long have you been in this role?" and "what's hitting you hardest?" These change which advice you get.

4

Don't stop at the first response

By the 3rd or 4th exchange, it starts seeing patterns you can't see yourself. That's where the real value is.

5

Ask it to push you

"Be honest. What am I avoiding here?"
"Don't sugarcoat this. What would you tell a friend?"

What Career OS is (and isn't)

Career OS is a free GPT-powered career advisor. It covers 19 real career situations, from first jobs to burnout to redundancy. It talks to you like a sharp, experienced friend. It won't sugarcoat. It won't send you off a cliff either.

Not a therapist.

If you're in genuine mental health crisis, please talk to a professional. Career OS will tell you this too.

Not a lawyer.

Employment law varies by country. It won't pretend to give legal advice.

Not magic.

It gives you clarity and a plan. You still have to do the hard part: have the conversation, make the decision, send the email.

Not generic.

It adapts to your experience level, your industry, and your specific situation. A 2-year professional gets different advice from a 15-year veteran.
