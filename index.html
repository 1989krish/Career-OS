How to talk to Career OS

1

Start with your situation, not a question

Don't ask "how do I negotiate a raise?" Instead, tell it what's happening:

"I've been in my role for 3 years, doing work above my level. My manager keeps saying there's no budget. I don't know if I should push harder or start looking elsewhere."

The more context you give, the more specific the advice gets.

2

Be honest, not polished

This isn't a job interview. If your manager is awful, say that. If you're scared, say that. If you've been avoiding something for months — say that. Career OS works best with the real version, not the professional version.

3

Answer its questions

It'll ask things like "how long have you been in this role?" and "what's hitting you hardest?" These aren't filler — they change which advice you get.

4

Don't stop at the first response

The first answer is a starting point. By the 3rd or 4th exchange, it starts seeing patterns you can't see yourself. That's where the real value is.

5

Ask it to push you

If you want the uncomfortable truth:

"Be honest — what am I avoiding here?"
"Don't sugarcoat this. What would you tell a friend in my situation?"
